Adaptations

  1. Submerged parts of the hydrophytes are covered with mucilage which protects the plants from decaying.
  2. The stems are spongy and flexible.They have a number of air spaces.The stems of water lily or lotus have large tunnels and holes for sir that help the plant to float.Sometimes,the stems are inflated to store large quantity of air.
  3. The leaves of submerged plants are long,narrow and ribbon-shaped.Such leaves allow water to pass through them easily.
  4. Floating leaves are large and flat.they have waxy upper surfaces to make them waterproof. They have stomata on the upper surfaces which are exposed to air.
  5. All hydrophytes have a special tissue called aerenchyma.It provides buoyancy and flexibility.Mechanical tissues are poorly developed.
  6. Roots are either absent or poorly developed.
